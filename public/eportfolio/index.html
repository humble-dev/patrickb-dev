Introduction to Web Fundamentals

IN 1430, we learned how to create web-sites using HTML, CSS, JavaScript and Bootstrap, a CSS framework. I created my own websites when I was a lot younger using Adobe Dreamweaver, which was a visual way of doing it, meaning I didn't know of the coding or scripting involved. I am a perfectionist by nature, and it took a while for me to warm up to the language of web programming. It was until I really started looking at blogs and tutorials outside of class that I became obsessed with the design culture surrounding it. Now, I see web more as a visual medium and as a canvas in which one can electronically display his or her talents. In November, I became obsessed with building a portfolio for the author, Railee Bradshaw, that I found myself so consumed by the project that I would skip meals and even sleep, and often times pushed aside all other work in order to find out how to fix a background image onto the screen or how to use JavaScript to animate a nav bar. I love the potential there is here as a platform for storytelling and to help other creators discover their unique voices.

  • my first home page I feel that I've come a long way in such short time. Here's where I learned HTML enough to make links and show images.
  • the resume Here I learned about tables and also how to upload a font. I find my choice of colors questionable now.
  • a map of constellations Here I learned how to create image maps. For my assignment I chose to do a small section of the night sky. I think that particular subject has a lot of creative implications.
  • some little boxer puppies In this assignment, I used CSS styles to make different kinds of little boxes (of little boxers) with a shout-out to my old friend Bowser.
  • tasty pizza co.'s online order form Here I learned how to create a simple web-based form for ordering a pizza online. I remember thinking that columns were impossible in HTML. And it turns out, CSS makes it a lot easier.
  • a halloween-themed guessing game The guessing game assignment was my first foray into JavaScript. It reminds me of Java, but is different enough that it made me extremely nauseous at first. I loved how this assignment turned out. The fun I had finding cute images to style the page would later influence the obsession I developed with web design.
  • dragonslay-tac-toe Getting the JavaScript to work for my tictactoe game was a monumental task, but I was able to fight throught it. I discovered while working on this assignment that I spend an colossal amount of time thinking about the 'look and feel' of a web page, but I am usually really satisfied if I can complete it.
  • simply me My first bootstrap page. I love CSS frameworks, and using Bootstrap inspired me to do my own research and I found that w3schools has their own responsive CSS which I learned how to use, and for this final project I have learned how to use Tachyons.
  • pbrad dot me This is a company page for a made up company that I would love to start or be a part of some day - simply a company that designs and develops websites. I love using CSS animations and color gradients to make page 'pop'.
  • rogue nasa Rogue Nasa is the name of a band that my friends and I wanted to start, but we were so busy that we could never get it together. This was the last Bootstrap assignment and I felt like my tastes for color and imagery finally started to come together.
  • railee bradshaw dot com A personal project that I did outside of school as a birthday gift for my wife, who is a writer. I skipped a lot of lunches and my grades really dipped a few classes, but I really loved the experience of making something like this. It is everything I look for in a project - finding images with personality, designing functional elements that not only look interesting, but also work properly. It's still a work in progress (I'd like to incorporate WordPress as completely as I can) but I'm pleased with how it turned out so far.

Top Ten Signs You've Been On The Internet For Too Long

  1. You need 3 semesters of Calculus to know how many tabs you have open.
  2. You think everybody knows what an 'I Ching' is because you like a super obscure website.
  3. Turn your neck to the right, and then to the left, look up, look down. That made a lot of noise, didn't it.
  4. You are reading YouTube comments even though they make you mad.
  5. You know about the "fractal corgi".
  6. It gives you anxiety to think of how many bookmarks you actually have in your browser that are not sorted at all.
  7. You start Google searching unique word combinations like 'mermaid corgi' and liked what you saw.
  8. You have intense opinions on the pronunciation of the word "gif".
  9. You have a sweet, android logo avatar version of yourself.
  10. You have the high score on the Google endless runner.
